VISION STATEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WAKEIT is a group accountability alarm platform. The core idea is simple: when
you know other people can see whether you woke up or not, you are far less likely
to sleep through your alarm.

The app allows a group Admin to schedule alarms for everyone in their group.
When the alarm fires, every member must solve a math problem to silence it —
there is no snooze button, no dismiss button, and no way to turn it off without
completing the challenge. The moment someone solves the challenge, everyone in
the group sees it in real time on the Group Dashboard.

This creates three powerful forces working together:
  1. Social pressure   — your group can see if you missed your alarm
  2. Cognitive barrier — you must be mentally awake enough to solve math
  3. Reliability       — the alarm works even without internet

The app is built for the Indian market, priced in INR, and designed for young
adults who want to build morning discipline but struggle with motivation alone.

SECTION 2: THE PROBLEM WE ARE SOLVING
================================================================================

Traditional alarm clocks have two fatal flaws:

FLAW 1: They are too easy to dismiss.
Hitting snooze or turning off an alarm takes zero mental effort. You can do it
while still half asleep. The alarm has done its job (making noise) but completely
failed at its purpose (getting you awake and out of bed).

FLAW 2: There are no consequences for failing.
Nobody knows if you slept through your alarm. There is no accountability. If you
were supposed to wake up at 6am for a workout with friends and you slept until
8am, the only person who knows is you — and you already decided to sleep in.

WAKEIT solves both problems:

SOLUTION 1: Math challenge as the off switch.
The alarm cannot be turned off without solving a math problem. Easy difficulty
requires single-digit addition/subtraction. Medium requires two-digit
multiplication. Hard requires multi-step equations. This forces the brain to
actually engage before the alarm stops. You cannot solve math while still asleep.

SOLUTION 2: Group visibility creates accountability.
When you complete the math challenge, your completion is broadcast to your entire
group in real time. When you miss it (timeout after 5 minutes), that is also
visible. Your Group Dashboard shows exactly who woke up and who did not. This
social visibility is the core product differentiator.

WHY THIS MATTERS FOR INDIA:
India has a massive population of young students and professionals who live in
group settings (hostel rooms, shared apartments, family homes) and who are deeply
motivated by social dynamics. Group accountability is culturally resonant.
The price points (Rs249/year for members, Rs1,249/year for admins) are
deliberately low to be accessible across income levels.

SECTION 3: HOW THE APP WORKS — BIG PICTURE
================================================================================

Here is the complete user journey from signup to alarm completion, written in
plain language so the agent understands the full flow before reading the details.

STEP 1 — SIGN UP & CHOOSE A PLAN
A new user downloads WAKEIT, sees 3 onboarding slides explaining what the app
does, then signs up with email and password. After signing up, they are shown
a plan comparison screen. They can purchase Member (Rs249/yr) or Admin
(Rs1,249/yr), or they can dismiss without purchasing to start a 3-day Free Trial
that gives them Admin-level access for 3 days.

STEP 2 — CREATE OR JOIN A GROUP
If the user is an Admin or on Free Trial, they can create a group. Creating a
group generates a unique 6-character invite code. They share this code with
people they want to wake up with — teammates, hostel friends, study group, gym
partners. Anyone with the code can join the group by entering it in the Join
Group screen (they need at least a Member plan or active Free Trial to join).

STEP 3 — ADMIN CREATES AN ALARM
The Admin opens their group, taps "Create Alarm", picks a date and time, chooses
difficulty (Easy / Medium / Hard), optionally uploads a custom sound or records
their voice, and saves it. The alarm is sent to Supabase, which then sends a
silent push notification (FCM) to every member's device. Each device writes the
alarm to local SQLite storage and schedules it with Android AlarmManager. This
happens silently in the background — members do not see a notification at this
point, but their devices are now ready to fire the alarm at the exact scheduled
time, even if they lose internet.

STEP 4 — THE ALARM FIRES
At the exact scheduled time, Android AlarmManager wakes the device and triggers
Notifee to display a full-screen alarm UI. This happens even if:
  - The app is completely closed (force-killed)
  - The device has no internet connection
  - The screen is off and the device is in Doze mode
  - The device was recently rebooted (Boot Receiver restores alarms on reboot)

The full-screen alarm UI opens over the lock screen. The volume ramps to maximum.
The device vibrates. There is no dismiss button. There is no snooze button. The
only way to stop the alarm is to complete the math challenge.

STEP 5 — MATH CHALLENGE
The user is shown a math problem based on the difficulty set by the Admin. They
type their answer on a numeric keypad. If they get it wrong, the challenge resets
and the alarm keeps ringing. If they get it right, the alarm stops, a success
screen is shown, and the completion is posted to Supabase.

STEP 6 — GROUP SEES THE RESULT
Via Supabase Realtime, the Group Dashboard for every member updates instantly to
show that this person completed the challenge. The completion time is recorded.
Members who miss the 5-minute window are automatically marked as "missed" and
that status is also visible to the group.

STEP 7 — STATS UPDATE
After each alarm, the member's wake score (percentage of alarms completed) and
streak (consecutive days completed) are recalculated and saved.

This is the complete product loop. Everything in this PRD supports this loop.

SECTION 4: USER TYPES & SUBSCRIPTION PLANS
================================================================================

WAKEIT has two types of users and three plan tiers.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.1 USER TYPES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ADMIN
An Admin is someone who creates and manages groups. They schedule alarms for
their group members. They can remove members from their group. They can see
per-member wake stats. An Admin also receives all alarms just like a member —
they are not exempt from the math challenge. Admin plan allows creating up to
5 groups with up to 20 members each and up to 20 alarms per group.

MEMBER
A Member is someone who joins groups created by Admins. They receive alarms,
complete math challenges, and can see the group dashboard. They cannot create
groups or schedule alarms. Member plan allows joining unlimited groups.

NOTE: An Admin plan user can ALSO join groups as a member. The Admin plan
includes all Member plan capabilities plus the ability to create groups.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.3 HOW PLAN ENFORCEMENT WORKS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

IMPORTANT: Plan limits are NEVER enforced on the client (the app). They are
ALWAYS enforced on the server inside Supabase Edge Functions. This means even
if someone modifies the app code or sends API requests directly, they cannot
bypass plan limits. An Edge Function validates the user's plan before performing
any action like creating a group or scheduling an alarm.

On app launch:
  1. App calls RevenueCat: Purchases.getCustomerInfo()
  2. RevenueCat returns the user's active entitlements
  3. App stores entitlements in Zustand authStore
  4. All plan-gated UI uses authStore — it never calls RevenueCat in real time
     during navigation (this would be slow and wasteful)

When a user purchases, cancels, or their plan expires:
  1. RevenueCat sends a webhook POST to the revenuecat-webhook Edge Function
  2. The webhook validates the secret in the Authorization header
  3. The webhook updates users.plan_type and user_subscriptions in Supabase
  4. On the next app launch, Purchases.getCustomerInfo() returns the updated state

iOS pricing note: Apple charges 30% commission vs Google's 15% after year 1.
When iOS launches, pricing will be reviewed. No decision is locked in at this
stage of development.

5.1 AUTHENTICATION & ONBOARDING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PURPOSE: Get a new user signed up and into the app as quickly as possible, with
the right plan selected and permissions granted.

--- SPLASH SCREEN ---
What it does: Shows when the app first launches. Displays the WAKEIT logo with
an animation. Runs a silent version check in the background (to alert users if
they are on an outdated version). After the animation, navigates to Onboarding
(first launch) or the main app (returning user who is logged in).

Do NOT show onboarding again if the user has already completed it. Store a
flag in local storage (AsyncStorage or SQLite) to track whether onboarding
has been seen.

--- ONBOARDING SLIDES (3 screens) ---
What it does: Shows 3 slides that explain what WAKEIT does before the user
signs up. Each slide has an illustration, a heading, and 1-2 sentences of
explanation. There is a "Skip" button (top right) that goes directly to Login,
and a "Next" button that advances slides. The final slide has a "Get Started"
button that goes to Sign Up.

Slide 1: "Wake up together" — explains group accountability concept
Slide 2: "No more snoozing" — explains the math challenge
Slide 3: "Always on time" — explains offline reliability

Only show onboarding on first launch. After the user completes it or skips,
never show it again on subsequent launches.

--- SIGN UP ---
What it does: Creates a new user account.

Fields:
  - Full Name (text input)
  - Email Address (email input with validation)
  - Password (password input, min 8 characters, show/hide toggle)
  - "Sign Up" button

On submit:
  1. Validate all fields are filled and email format is correct
  2. Show loading state on the Sign Up button
  3. Call Supabase Auth: supabase.auth.signUp({ email, password, options: { data: { full_name } } })
  4. On success: navigate to Plan Selection screen
  5. On error: show inline error message (email already in use, weak password, etc.)

After signup, a record is automatically created in the public.users table via
a Supabase database trigger (trigger fires on auth.users insert, copies user
data to public.users and sets trial_started_at and trial_ends_at).

--- PLAN SELECTION ---
What it does: Shows the two paid plans side by side with a comparison of
features. This appears immediately after Sign Up. The user can purchase a plan
OR dismiss to start the Free Trial.

Content to show for each plan:
  Member Plan (Rs249/year): Join unlimited groups, receive alarms, complete
  challenges, view group stats
  Admin Plan (Rs1,249/year): Everything in Member, PLUS create up to 5 groups,
  schedule alarms, manage members, set alarm difficulty

Buttons:
  - "Choose Member" → triggers RevenueCat purchase flow for wakeit_member_annual
  - "Choose Admin" → triggers RevenueCat purchase flow for wakeit_admin_annual
  - "Start Free Trial" (smaller, below plans) → dismisses screen, starts 3-day trial

On purchase success: navigate to Home Dashboard
On trial start: navigate to Home Dashboard, set trial flag in Supabase

RevenueCat purchase flow is handled by the RevenueCat SDK. The app calls
Purchases.purchasePackage() and RevenueCat handles the Google Play dialog,
receipt validation, and webhook to Supabase.

There must also be a "Restore Purchases" button (small, at the bottom of the
screen) for users who reinstall the app and need to restore their subscription.

--- LOGIN ---
What it does: Authenticates a returning user.

Fields:
  - Email Address
  - Password (with show/hide toggle)
  - "Log In" button
  - "Forgot Password?" link (below the button)
  - "Don't have an account? Sign Up" link (at the bottom)

On submit:
  1. Validate fields are not empty
  2. Show loading state
  3. Call: supabase.auth.signInWithPassword({ email, password })
  4. On success: navigate to Home Dashboard (or wherever the user was going)
  5. On error: show specific error messages:
     - Wrong password: "Incorrect password. Please try again."
     - Email not found: "No account found with this email."
     - Email not verified: "Please verify your email before logging in."
     - Network error: "Connection error. Please check your internet."

--- FORGOT PASSWORD ---
What it does: Sends a password reset email via Supabase.

Field:
  - Email Address
  - "Send Reset Email" button
  - "Back to Login" link

On submit:
  1. Validate email format
  2. Call: supabase.auth.resetPasswordForEmail(email)
  3. Show success message: "Check your email for a password reset link."
  4. Do NOT tell the user if the email exists or not (security best practice)

--- PERMISSION REQUIRED SCREEN ---
What it does: Shown contextually before requesting any device permission. Never
shown on first launch without context. Always explains WHY the permission
is needed before asking for it.

When to show it:
  - Before requesting notification permission: shown when user first tries to
    receive an alarm (explain: "WAKEIT needs notification access to ring your
    alarm")
  - Before requesting microphone permission: shown when user taps "Record Voice
    Alarm" (explain: "We need microphone access to record your voice alarm")
  - Before requesting storage permission: shown when user taps "Upload Audio

Layout:
  - Icon representing the permission type
  - Clear heading: "Allow Notifications" / "Allow Microphone" / "Allow Storage"
  - 2-3 sentence explanation in plain language of why this permission is needed
  - "Allow" button → triggers the system permission dialog
  - "Not Now" button → dismisses without requesting (feature will not work)

If the user previously denied a permission, the "Allow" button should open
the device Settings app directly (using Linking.openSettings()) since Android

5.2 GROUP MANAGEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PURPOSE: Allow Admins to create groups and Members to join them. Groups are the
core unit of social accountability in WAKEIT.

--- CREATE GROUP (Admin only) ---
What it does: Creates a new group with a unique invite code.

Fields:
  - Group Name (required, text input, max 50 characters)
  - Group Description (optional, text area, max 200 characters)
  - Default Alarm Sound (optional — opens Sound Selection screen to pick a
    default sound for all alarms in this group)
  - "Create Group" button

Validation (handled in Edge Function, not client):
  - User must be Admin plan or active Free Trial
  - User must not have reached their group creation limit (5 for Admin, 2 for Trial)
  - Group name cannot be empty

On submit:
  1. POST to create-group Edge Function with { name, description, default_sound_url }
  2. Edge Function generates a unique 6-character alphanumeric invite code
     (e.g. "A3X9KM"). The code is case-insensitive but stored in uppercase.
  3. Edge Function inserts into groups table
  4. Also inserts a group_members row for the Admin themselves (so they are
     a member of their own group)
  5. Returns { group_id, invite_code }
  6. App navigates to Group Dashboard for the new group
  7. Show the invite code prominently on the Group Dashboard so the Admin can
     share it immediately

The invite code is auto-generated. The Admin cannot choose their own code.

--- JOIN GROUP ---
What it does: Adds a user to an existing group using an invite code.

Fields:
  - Invite Code input (6 characters, auto-capitalizes, shows characters as
    user types — do NOT auto-submit, wait for "Join" button)
  - "Join Group" button
  - "Cancel" link

Validation (handled in Edge Function):
  - User must have a valid plan (Member, Admin, or active Free Trial)
  - Invite code must exist and belong to an active group
  - Group must not have reached its member limit (20 for Admin groups)
  - User must not already be a member of this group

On submit:
  1. POST to join-group Edge Function with { invite_code }
  2. Edge Function validates all conditions above
  3. On success: inserts group_members row, sends notification to Admin
  4. App navigates to Group Dashboard for the joined group
  5. On error: show specific error message:
     - Invalid code: "This invite code doesn't exist. Check it and try again."
     - Group full: "This group is full."
     - Already member: "You're already in this group."
     - Plan required: "You need a plan to join groups. Tap here to upgrade."

--- GROUPS LIST SCREEN ---
What it does: Shows all groups the user is in (both created and joined).

Layout:
  - "Create Group" button at top (only visible if user is Admin or on Trial)
  - "Join Group" button at top (visible to all users)
  - List of Group Cards, each showing:
    - Group name
    - Number of members
    - Next scheduled alarm time (or "No upcoming alarms")
    - Admin's name (or "You are the Admin" if user is the admin)
    - An indicator for unread notifications related to this group

If the user has no groups: show empty state with illustration and text
"You're not in any groups yet. Create one or ask for an invite code."

--- GROUP DASHBOARD ---
What it does: The main screen for a group. Shows alarms, member status, and
group stats. This is the screen where real-time updates happen.

This screen has multiple sections:

SECTION 1 — NEXT ALARM COUNTDOWN
If there is a scheduled alarm within the next 24 hours, show a large countdown
timer (HH:MM:SS format) with the alarm name and time. This timer counts down
in real time.

SECTION 2 — ALARMS LIST
Shows all scheduled and recent alarms for this group. Each alarm card shows:
  - Alarm name/title
  - Date and time (in user's local timezone)
  - Difficulty level (Easy / Medium / Hard)
  - Recurring indicator (if it repeats)
  - Status (Scheduled / Active / Completed / Cancelled)
  - For Admin: Edit and Delete buttons

"Add Alarm" button (only visible to Admin).

SECTION 3 — MEMBER STATUS GRID
A grid of member cards showing each member's status for the MOST RECENT alarm.
Each card shows:
  - Member name
  - Wake Score percentage
  - Current streak (number of consecutive days completed)
  - Status for latest alarm:
    Green = Completed (shows completion time)
    Orange = Pending (alarm hasn't fired yet or they haven't completed it)
    Red = Missed (timeout expired without completion)

This section updates in REAL TIME via Supabase Realtime. When a member
completes the math challenge, their card immediately changes from Orange to
Green without the user needing to refresh the screen.

SECTION 4 — GROUP INFO
  - Group name and description
  - Invite code (shown for Admin, tap to copy)
  - "View All Members" button → navigates to Group Members screen

--- GROUP MEMBERS SCREEN ---
What it does: Shows a detailed list of all group members with their stats.

For each member:
  - Profile photo (if available) or initials avatar
  - Full name
  - Wake Score (0-100%)
  - Current streak (consecutive days)
  - Longest streak ever
  - Member since date (joined_at)
  - Total alarms received
  - Total alarms completed

Admin-only action on each member card:
  - "Remove Member" button → shows confirmation dialog before removing

Confirmation dialog: "Remove [Name] from [Group Name]? They will lose access to
all group alarms." with "Remove" (red) and "Cancel" buttons.

On remove:
  1. POST to remove-member Edge Function
  2. Edge Function sets group_members.is_active = false
  3. Edge Function sends FCM push to the removed member's device
  4. The removed member's app receives the FCM, cancels all AlarmManager entries
     for this group, and deletes them from local SQLite
  5. Group member count updates
  6. Removed member is no longer shown in the list

--- GROUP STATISTICS ---
Available on the Group Dashboard, not a separate screen. Shows:
  - Group overall completion rate % (total completed / total alarms for all members)
  - Individual member current streak
  - Individual member wake score

Full leaderboard and monthly summary are OUT OF SCOPE for v1.0.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.3 ALARM SYSTEM — CRITICAL PATH
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PURPOSE: The alarm system is the most important and most complex part of WAKEIT.
Every component must work perfectly. An alarm that fails to ring is a broken
product. Understand this section completely before writing any alarm-related code.

The alarm system has two completely separate parts that must work together:
  PART A: Cloud side (Supabase + FCM) — delivers alarm data to devices
  PART B: Device side (SQLite + AlarmManager + Notifee) — rings the alarm

These two parts are connected by FCM silent push notifications. Once Part B
receives the data from Part A and schedules the alarm locally, Part B works
completely independently of Part A. The alarm will ring even if Supabase goes
down, FCM goes down, or the user has no internet.

--- ALARM CREATION (Admin action) ---

Screen: Create Alarm

Fields:
  - Alarm Title (required, e.g. "Morning Wake Up", "Study Session", max 50 chars)
  - Date (date picker, must be future date)
  - Time (time picker, 12-hour format with AM/PM, converts to 24-hour internally)
  - Difficulty (segmented control: Easy / Medium / Hard)
  - Alarm Sound (shows currently selected sound with "Change" button that opens
    Sound Selection screen)
  - Recurring toggle (off by default)
    If ON, shows:
    - Day selector (Mon, Tue, Wed, Thu, Fri, Sat, Sun checkboxes — at least
      one must be selected)
    - End date picker (required if recurring, max 30 days from today)
  - "Save Alarm" button

Validation:
  - All required fields must be filled
  - Date + time must be at least 2 minutes in the future
  - At least one day selected if recurring

On save:
  1. App sends POST request to create-alarm Edge Function
  2. Edge Function (see Section 9.4) handles everything from here
  3. Show loading state on button until response received
  4. On success: navigate back to Group Dashboard, show new alarm in list
  5. On error: show specific error message and keep user on Create Alarm screen

ALARM TIME STORAGE: All alarm times are stored in UTC in Supabase. The app
converts to the user's local timezone for display. When syncing to devices via
FCM, the alarm_time_utc is sent. The device converts it to local time for
AlarmManager scheduling and for display.

--- ALARM EDITING (Admin action) ---

Screen: Edit Alarm (same layout as Create Alarm, fields pre-filled)

Rules:
  - An alarm can only be edited up to 1 minute before its scheduled time
  - If the alarm is within 1 minute, show: "This alarm rings soon and cannot
    be edited."
  - Editing sends an update via update-alarm Edge Function
  - Edge Function sends FCM alarm_update push to all members
  - All member devices cancel the old AlarmManager entry and schedule a new one

--- ALARM DELETION (Admin action) ---

The Admin can delete an alarm from the Group Dashboard by tapping the Delete
button on an alarm card.

Confirmation dialog: "Delete [Alarm Title]? All members will be notified."
with "Delete" (red) and "Cancel" buttons.

On confirm:
  1. POST to delete-alarm Edge Function
  2. Edge Function sets alarm status = cancelled in Supabase
  3. Edge Function sends FCM alarm_cancel push to all member devices
  4. Member devices cancel their AlarmManager entry and remove from SQLite
  5. Alarm card is removed from the Group Dashboard
  6. All pending alarm_completion records for this alarm are updated to cancelled

--- ALARM DETAIL SCREEN ---

Shows full information about one alarm:
  - Alarm title, date, time (in local timezone), difficulty, sound
  - Recurring schedule (if applicable)
  - Member completion grid (same as Group Dashboard but for this specific alarm)
  - For Admin: Edit button and Delete button

--- ALARM COUNTDOWN ---

On the Group Dashboard, if there is a scheduled alarm within the next 24 hours,
show a countdown timer in HH:MM:SS format that updates every second. This is
calculated purely on the client — it does not need a server call. The app
subtracts the current time from the alarm_time_local stored for that alarm.

--- RECURRING ALARMS ---

When a recurring alarm is created, it is stored as a single record in Supabase
with is_recurring = true, recurrence_days = [1,3,5] (for Mon/Wed/Fri, where
0=Sun, 1=Mon ... 6=Sat), and recurrence_end_date = the end date.

On the device side, when the FCM sync is received for a recurring alarm, the
device schedules AlarmManager entries for ALL occurrences within the recurrence
window (up to 30 days). Each occurrence is a separate AlarmManager entry with
a unique identifier derived from alarm_id + occurrence_date.

After each ring, the device automatically schedules the NEXT occurrence if it
is within the recurrence window. If the next occurrence is beyond the end date,
no more scheduling happens.

--- ALARM STATUS VALUES ---

scheduled  : Alarm is set and will fire in the future
active     : Alarm is currently ringing (between fire time and 5-minute timeout)
completed  : All members have either completed or missed (5-minute window closed)
cancelled  : Admin deleted this alarm

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.4 MATH CHALLENGE — WAKE VERIFICATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PURPOSE: Force the user to be cognitively engaged before they can stop the alarm.
This is the mechanism that makes WAKEIT different from a regular alarm clock.

--- FULL SCREEN ALARM UI ---

When AlarmManager fires, the following happens in this exact sequence:
  1. Android AlarmManager triggers the AlarmReceiver (native Java)
  2. AlarmReceiver calls Notifee to display a high-priority full-screen notification
     on the wakeit_alarm channel
  3. The AlarmScreen React Native component opens as a full-screen intent
  4. Android window flags are set: FLAG_SHOW_WHEN_LOCKED, FLAG_TURN_SCREEN_ON,
     FLAG_KEEP_SCREEN_ON — this makes the screen turn on even if locked
  5. Volume is programmatically set to maximum
  6. Alarm sound starts playing (loops continuously)
  7. Vibration pattern starts

The AlarmScreen shows:
  - Large time display (current time)
  - Group name and alarm title
  - A message like "Wake up! Solve the challenge to stop the alarm."
  - A button "I'm Awake — Show Challenge" that navigates to the Math Challenge screen

There is NO dismiss button. There is NO snooze button. The back button is
disabled on this screen. The home button is intercepted where possible on Android
(though Android may not allow fully preventing home press — the alarm will
continue ringing in the foreground service even if the user navigates away).

--- MATH CHALLENGE SCREEN ---

The Math Challenge screen shows one math problem and a numeric keypad.

EASY (single-digit addition/subtraction):
  Examples: "3 + 5 = ?", "9 - 4 = ?", "7 + 2 = ?"
  Answer is always a positive integer, max 2 digits

MEDIUM (two-digit multiplication):
  Examples: "12 × 7 = ?", "8 × 13 = ?", "15 × 6 = ?"
  Answer is always a positive integer, max 3 digits

HARD (multi-step equations):
  Examples: "(4 + 3) × 8 - 5 = ?", "6² + (12 - 7) = ?", "3 × (9 + 4) - 11 = ?"
  Answer is always a positive integer, max 4 digits

The problem is generated randomly on the device — it does not come from the
server. The difficulty level is synced from the alarm data.

The numeric keypad has digits 0-9 and a backspace button. There is no keyboard —
only the custom numeric keypad. There is NO submit button. The answer is checked
automatically when the correct number of digits has been entered (for Easy, when
the user enters a 1 or 2 digit answer and it matches — but actually use a Submit
button to avoid accidental submission).

Use a Submit button. The user types their answer on the keypad, then taps Submit.

ON WRONG ANSWER:
  - Show brief visual feedback (red flash on the input field)
  - Haptic feedback (error pattern)
  - Clear the input field
  - Show new math problem (generate a NEW problem, not the same one)
  - Alarm continues ringing
  - Increment the attempts counter (stored locally, sent to Supabase on completion)

ON CORRECT ANSWER:
  - Stop alarm sound immediately
  - Stop vibration immediately
  - Haptic feedback (success pattern)
  - Navigate to Challenge Success screen
  - In the background: POST to complete-challenge Edge Function with
    { alarm_id, attempts: N, completed_at: timestamp }

TIMEOUT (5 minutes from alarm_time, not from when the screen opened):
  - If the math challenge is NOT completed within 5 minutes of the original
    alarm time, the alarm is automatically marked as missed
  - Stop alarm sound
  - Stop vibration
  - Navigate to Alarm Missed screen
  - In the background: POST to complete-challenge Edge Function with
    { alarm_id, status: 'missed' }

HOW TO CALCULATE TIMEOUT:
  The 5-minute window starts at alarm_time (the scheduled alarm time), NOT
  when the user tapped "I'm Awake". Store alarm_time when AlarmManager fires.
  Check: if (current_time - alarm_time) > 5 * 60 * 1000, show timeout.
  Run a timer that checks this every second on the Math Challenge screen.

--- CHALLENGE SUCCESS SCREEN ---

Shows:
  - Green checkmark animation
  - "Challenge Complete!" heading
  - The time they completed it (e.g., "Completed at 6:03 AM")
  - Their current wake score and streak (fetched from Supabase after posting result)
  - "Go to Group Dashboard" button
  - "Go to Home" button

--- ALARM MISSED SCREEN ---

Shows:
  - Red X animation
  - "Alarm Missed" heading
  - The alarm time that was missed
  - A brief motivational message (e.g., "Tomorrow is a new chance. Set up
    accountability to stay on track.")
  - "Go to Group Dashboard" button
  - "Go to Home" button

5.5 CUSTOM ALARM SOUNDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PURPOSE: Allow Admins to make their group's alarm more personal and harder to
ignore by using a custom sound (like a friend's voice saying "WAKE UP!").

--- SOUND SELECTION SCREEN ---

Shown when Admin taps "Change Sound" on Create Alarm or Edit Alarm screens.

Three tabs or sections:
  1. PRESET SOUNDS — list of default alarm sounds bundled with the app
     (at least 5 options). Tap to preview. Tap again to select.
  2. UPLOAD FILE — "Upload Audio File" button opens device file picker
     filtered to MP3 and WAV files. Max file size 5MB. On selection,
     show file name and a preview play button. Show upload progress bar.
  3. RECORD VOICE — "Start Recording" button. Requests RECORD_AUDIO permission
     first (via Permission Required screen). Shows recording timer while
     recording. "Stop Recording" button ends recording. Shows playback control
     to preview the recording. Save button uploads the recording.

On selecting/uploading/recording:
  1. Upload audio file to Supabase Storage in the 'audio' bucket
     Path: audio/{group_id}/{alarm_id}/{filename}
  2. Get the public URL from Supabase Storage
  3. Store the URL in the alarm's sound_url field (or group's default_sound_url)
  4. The FCM alarm_sync payload includes this sound_url
  5. Each member device downloads and caches the audio file locally on receipt
     of the FCM message (before the alarm fires, so it works offline)
  6. The cached local file path is stored in local_alarms.sound_path

For preset sounds: sound_path stores the bundled asset name (no download needed).

If a custom sound fails to load at alarm time, fall back to the default system
alarm sound. Never let a failed sound prevent the alarm from ringing.

--- SUPABASE STORAGE LIMITS ---

The Supabase free tier allows 1GB total storage. At 5MB max per file, this means
the app can store up to ~200 custom sounds before hitting the limit. This is
sufficient for early launch. Monitor storage usage in Supabase dashboard.
If approaching limits, implement cleanup of old/deleted alarm sounds.

5.6 PERFORMANCE & STATS (SIMPLIFIED)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PURPOSE: Give users a sense of progress and accountability through visible stats.
Note: Full leaderboard and monthly summary are OUT OF SCOPE for v1.0.

--- WAKE SCORE ---

The wake score is a percentage calculated as:
  wake_score = (total_completed / total_alarms_received) * 100

This is stored in group_members.wake_score and updated by the complete-challenge
Edge Function after every alarm (whether completed or missed).

The wake score is per group — a user can have different scores in different groups.

Display: Show as a percentage (e.g., "87%") on member cards on the Group
Dashboard and Group Members screen.

--- STREAK ---

The streak counts consecutive days on which the user completed ALL alarms they
received across the group.

  current_streak: how many days in a row they have completed alarms (resets to 0
  if they miss even one alarm on a given day)

  longest_streak: the maximum streak they have ever achieved

Stored in group_members: current_streak, longest_streak.

Updated by complete-challenge Edge Function:
  - If the user completes the alarm on a day they also completed all previous
    alarms, increment current_streak
  - If the user misses an alarm, reset current_streak to 0
  - If current_streak > longest_streak, update longest_streak

Display: Show "🔥 5 day streak" on member cards.

--- WHERE STATS APPEAR ---

  - Group Dashboard → Member status grid → each card shows wake score + streak
  - Group Members screen → detailed card for each member
  - Profile screen → user's own stats across all their groups

5.7 NOTIFICATIONS

   Recipient: All active group members
   Title: "Alarm updated in [Group Name]"

   Trigger: Admin deletes an alarm
   Recipient: All active group members
   Title: "Alarm cancelled in [Group Name]"
   Body: "[Alarm Title] scheduled for [time] has been cancelled."

5. MEMBER JOINED
   Trigger: A new member joins the group (via invite code)
   Recipient: Group Admin only
   Title: "New member in [Group Name]"

   Recipient: The removed member

   Admin notification title: "[Member Name] missed [Alarm Title]"

--- NOTIFICATION HISTORY SCREEN ---

SECTION 8: UI BUILD PROCESS (ANTIGRAVITY WORKFLOW)
================================================================================

PURPOSE: Define exactly how the Antigravity AI coding agent should receive and
build UI designs. This ensures consistent, faithful implementation.

--- THE PROCESS ---

STEP 1: The designer (product owner) attaches 5 screen design images to the
Antigravity chat.

STEP 2: Antigravity reads the design images carefully and builds all 5 screens
EXACTLY as shown in the designs. This means:
  - Exact colors (use the color tokens from Section 7 — if the design shows
    a green button, it must be #4CA771, not a different shade of green)
  - Exact typography (Poppins, exact sizes from Section 7)
  - Exact component layout (position of every element on screen)
  - Exact spacing (use 8px grid from Section 7)
  - Exact icon choices (Lucide icons as specified)
  - Exact copy (text content visible in the design)

STEP 3: After building the 5 screens, Antigravity confirms completion and asks
for the next batch.

STEP 4: The designer reviews the built screens. If any screen does not match
the design, corrections are requested before proceeding to the next batch.

STEP 5: The next 5 design images are attached and the cycle repeats until all
27 screens are built.

--- RULES FOR ANTIGRAVITY WHEN BUILDING UI ---

1. DO NOT invent UI elements that are not in the design. If the design does not
   show a button, do not add one. If the design shows 3 items in a list, build
   the component to render a list (not hardcoded 3 items).

2. DO NOT change colors. The design is authoritative. Use exact hex values.

3. DO make components dynamic. If the design shows a group name, it should come
   from a prop/state, not be hardcoded as "My Study Group".

4. DO implement the complete design including: header, content, bottom navigation
   (where applicable), loading states, and empty states.

5. DO connect screens to real data where the API is already built. If the
   Group Dashboard design is being built and the Supabase tables + Edge Functions
   already exist, connect them — do not use mock data.

6. IF a detail in the design is unclear (e.g., a color looks ambiguous, an icon
   is not identifiable), ask before making an assumption.

7. FOLLOW Section 7 design tokens for anything not explicitly shown in the
   design images (e.g., if a new button type appears, use the Primary Button
   spec from Section 7).

8. KEYBOARD AVOIDANCE: For all screens with input fields, use KeyboardAvoidingView
   with behavior="height" (this is the correct value for Android).
   When iOS is added later, behavior="padding" is used for iOS.

SECTION 9: TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9.1 TECHNOLOGY STACK
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Every technology choice is listed here with the reason it was chosen.

REACT NATIVE + EXPO SDK 51+ (Bare Workflow)
  What: Cross-platform mobile framework
  Why: Allows building for Android (and later iOS) from one codebase
  Why bare workflow (not managed): We need native Android code for AlarmManager
  and BootReceiver. Expo managed workflow does not support writing native Java.

TYPESCRIPT (strict mode)
  What: Typed superset of JavaScript
  Why: Catches bugs at compile time. All files use .ts or .tsx extensions.
  Strict mode means no implicit any types, strict null checks enabled.

EXPO ROUTER (file-based navigation)
  What: File-system based routing for React Native (like Next.js for mobile)
  Why: Clean URL-like navigation, automatic deep linking, easy tab setup
  How: Files in /app directory automatically become screens

ZUSTAND (state management)
  What: Lightweight global state management library
  Why: Simpler than Redux, no boilerplate, works well with TypeScript
  Three stores: authStore (user + entitlements), groupStore (groups + members),
  alarmStore (alarms + completions)

SUPABASE (backend-as-a-service)
  What: Open source Firebase alternative with PostgreSQL
  Why: Provides database, auth, realtime, storage, and edge functions in one
  Cost: Free tier is used. Limits: 500MB DB, 1GB storage, 2 req/sec on Edge Functions
  Important: Start on free tier. Monitor usage. Upgrade at ~500 active users.

SUPABASE AUTH
  What: Authentication service built into Supabase
  Why: Handles email/password auth, JWT generation, password reset — no custom auth needed
  The JWT is included in all API calls to Supabase automatically by the JS client

POSTGRESQL (via Supabase)
  What: The database
  Why: Supabase is built on PostgreSQL. Provides SQL queries, triggers, RLS.
  Row Level Security is enabled on every table (see Section 9.11)

SUPABASE REALTIME
  What: WebSocket-based real-time data subscriptions
  Why: Needed for the Group Dashboard to update live when members complete challenges
  How: Subscribes to PostgreSQL table changes and pushes to connected clients

SUPABASE STORAGE
  What: File storage service
  Why: Stores custom alarm sounds uploaded by Admins
  Bucket: 'audio' bucket for all alarm sound files

SUPABASE EDGE FUNCTIONS (Deno runtime)
  What: Serverless functions that run on the edge
  Why: All critical writes (create alarm, complete challenge, etc.) must be
  validated server-side. Client cannot bypass plan limits. Edge Functions run
  TypeScript/Deno.

FIREBASE CLOUD MESSAGING (FCM v1 API)
  What: Google's push notification service
  Why: Industry standard for Android push notifications. Free.
  Two types of FCM messages used:
    1. Silent data messages (for alarm sync — no visible notification)
    2. Display notifications (for social notifications like "New alarm added")
  Note: Firebase account is already created. google-services.json must be
  placed in /android/app/ before building.

NOTIFEE
  What: React Native notification library
  Why: React Native's built-in notifications cannot do full-screen alarms,
  cannot override Do Not Disturb, and cannot wake the screen. Notifee can.
  Used for: Full-screen alarm display, wakeit_alarm channel, wakeit_social channel

ANDROID ALARMMANAGER (native)
  What: Android OS alarm scheduling API
  Why: Only the OS-level AlarmManager can guarantee an alarm fires at an exact
  time even when the app is closed and the device is in Doze mode.
  Specifically uses: setExactAndAllowWhileIdle() — fires even in Doze mode
  Requires: SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARM permission (Android 12+) and
  USE_EXACT_ALARM permission (Android 13+)
  Implementation: Native Java classes AlarmReceiver.java and BootReceiver.java
  are written in the /android/app/src/main/java/ directory

EXPO SQLITE
  What: SQLite database on the device
  Why: Alarms must work offline. SQLite stores a local copy of all scheduled
  alarms so AlarmManager can be scheduled without any network access.
  The local_alarms table is the offline safety net.

REVENUECAT SDK v7+
  What: Subscription management platform
  Why: Handles all complexities of Google Play Billing — receipt validation,
  entitlement management, renewal handling, refund handling
  Cost: Free up to $2,500/month tracked revenue. Sufficient for launch.
  The app never talks to Google Play Billing directly. It always goes through RC.

REACT NATIVE PAPER + CUSTOM COMPONENTS
  What: Material Design component library + custom WAKEIT design components
  Why: Provides base components (Modal, FAB, etc.) that are extended with the
  WAKEIT design system

EXPO GOOGLE FONTS — POPPINS
  What: Google Fonts Poppins loaded via Expo
  Why: Poppins is the brand typeface. All weights must be loaded on startup.

EXPO VECTOR ICONS + LUCIDE REACT NATIVE
  What: Icon libraries
  Why: Comprehensive icon sets that cover all needed icons consistently

EXPO EAS BUILD (free tier)
  What: Cloud build service for React Native apps
  Why: Builds Android .aab and .apk files without requiring a local development
  machine capable of running Android Studio and Gradle in production mode.
  Also builds iOS .ipa when iOS is added.

9.4 SUPABASE EDGE FUNCTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

There are 7 active Edge Functions in v1.0. All Edge Functions:
  - Are written in TypeScript (Deno runtime)
  - Require a valid Supabase Auth JWT in the Authorization header
  - Return JSON responses with { success: true, data: {...} } or { error: "message" }
  - Use the Supabase service role key internally (not the anon key) to bypass RLS
    for writes that the Edge Function has already validated
  - Log errors to Supabase Edge Function logs

--- EDGE FUNCTION 1: create-alarm ---

URL: POST /functions/v1/create-alarm

Request body:
{
  "group_id": "uuid",
  "title": "Morning Wake Up",
  "alarm_time": "2026-06-15T01:00:00Z",  // UTC ISO 8601
  "difficulty": "medium",
  "sound_url": "https://...",             // optional, null if using group default
  "is_recurring": false,
  "recurrence_days": null,                // or [1,3,5] for Mon/Wed/Fri
  "recurrence_end_date": null             // or "2026-07-15"
}

What it does:
  1. Extract user_id from JWT
  2. Verify user is the Admin of group_id
  3. Check Admin plan limits: count existing alarms for this group, if >= 20 return error
  4. Insert row into alarms table
  5. Fetch all active members of this group from group_members
     (where group_id = ? AND is_active = true)
  6. For each active member, insert an alarm_completions row:
     { alarm_id, user_id, group_id, status: 'pending' }
  7. Fetch FCM tokens for all active members from device_tokens
     (where user_id IN (member_ids) AND is_active = true)
  8. Build FCM silent data payload:
     {
       "type": "alarm_sync",
       "alarm_id": "uuid",
       "alarm_time_utc": "2026-06-15T01:00:00Z",
       "difficulty": "medium",
       "sound_url": "https://...",
       "group_id": "uuid",
       "group_name": "Study Group",
       "alarm_title": "Morning Wake Up"
     }
  9. Send FCM message to all device tokens via FCM v1 API
     (send individually or in batches of up to 500 — FCM supports multicast)
  10. Insert notification log records for each member (type: 'alarm_created')
  11. Return { success: true, alarm_id: "uuid" }

Error cases:
  - Not group admin → 403: "You must be the group admin to create alarms"
  - Alarm limit reached → 403: "You've reached the alarm limit for this group"
  - Invalid alarm time (past) → 400: "Alarm time must be in the future"

--- EDGE FUNCTION 2: update-alarm ---

URL: POST /functions/v1/update-alarm

Request body:
{
  "alarm_id": "uuid",
  "title": "Updated Title",            // optional
  "alarm_time": "2026-06-15T02:00:00Z", // optional, must be future
  "difficulty": "hard",                // optional
  "sound_url": "https://...",          // optional
  "is_recurring": false,               // optional
  "recurrence_days": null,             // optional
  "recurrence_end_date": null          // optional
}

What it does:
  1. Extract user_id from JWT
  2. Fetch alarm by alarm_id, verify it exists and status = 'scheduled'
  3. Verify user is the Admin of the alarm's group
  4. Verify alarm is more than 1 minute in the future
  5. Update the alarms table row with the new values
  6. Build FCM alarm_update payload:
     { "type": "alarm_update", "alarm_id": "uuid", "alarm_time_utc": "...",
       "difficulty": "hard", "sound_url": "...", "alarm_title": "..." }
  7. Fetch all active member FCM tokens for this group
  8. Send FCM to all devices
  9. Insert notification log records (type: 'alarm_updated')
  10. Return { success: true }

Error cases:
  - Not group admin → 403
  - Alarm not found or not scheduled → 404
  - Less than 1 minute to alarm → 400: "Alarm rings too soon to edit"

--- EDGE FUNCTION 3: delete-alarm ---

URL: POST /functions/v1/delete-alarm

Request body:
{
  "alarm_id": "uuid"
}

What it does:
  1. Extract user_id from JWT
  2. Fetch alarm, verify it exists
  3. Verify user is Admin of alarm's group
  4. Update alarms table: status = 'cancelled'
  5. Update all alarm_completions for this alarm: status = 'cancelled'
  6. Build FCM alarm_cancel payload:
     { "type": "alarm_cancel", "alarm_id": "uuid" }
  7. Fetch all active member FCM tokens
  8. Send FCM to all devices
  9. Insert notification log records (type: 'alarm_cancelled')
  10. Return { success: true }

Error cases:
  - Not group admin → 403
  - Alarm not found → 404

--- EDGE FUNCTION 4: complete-challenge ---

URL: POST /functions/v1/complete-challenge

Request body:
{
  "alarm_id": "uuid",
  "status": "completed",      // or "missed"
  "completed_at": "2026-06-15T01:03:22Z",  // UTC, only if status = completed
  "attempts": 2               // number of wrong answers before correct one
}

What it does:
  1. Extract user_id from JWT
  2. Find alarm_completion row: alarm_id = ? AND user_id = ? AND status = 'pending'
  3. Update alarm_completion:
     - status = 'completed' or 'missed'
     - completed_at = value from request (if completed)
     - attempts = value from request
     - challenge_difficulty = difficulty from alarm record (snapshot)
  4. Fetch current group_members stats for this user in this group
  5. Recalculate wake_score:
     total_completed = existing + (1 if completed else 0)
     total_alarms_received = existing + 1
     wake_score = (total_completed / total_alarms_received) * 100
  6. Update streak:
     If completed:
       Check if user completed all alarms from yesterday (to maintain streak)
       If yes: current_streak = current_streak + 1
       If no (streak broken then resumed): current_streak = 1
     If missed:
       current_streak = 0
     If current_streak > longest_streak: longest_streak = current_streak
  7. Update group_members row with new wake_score, total_completed,
     total_alarms_received, current_streak, longest_streak
  8. If status = 'completed', send FCM notification to Admin:
     { type: 'challenge_complete', member_name: "...", alarm_title: "...",
       completed_at: "..." }
  9. Insert notification log records
  10. Return { success: true, wake_score: 87, current_streak: 5 }

Error cases:
  - No pending completion found → 404 (may already have been completed)
  - Invalid status value → 400

--- EDGE FUNCTION 5: join-group ---

URL: POST /functions/v1/join-group

Request body:
{
  "invite_code": "A3X9KM"
}

What it does:
  1. Extract user_id from JWT
  2. Look up group by invite_code where is_active = true
  3. If not found: return 404 "Invalid invite code"
  4. Verify user has a valid plan:
     - plan_type = 'member' or 'admin' or (plan_type = 'free_trial' AND trial_ends_at > now())
     - If not: return 403 "You need a plan to join groups"
  5. Check if user is already a member (group_members row with is_active = true)
     - If yes: return 400 "You're already in this group"
  6. Check member count against plan limit (20 for Admin plan groups)
     - If full: return 403 "This group is full"
  7. Insert group_members row:
     { group_id, user_id, is_active: true, wake_score: 0, ... }
  8. Update groups.member_count + 1
  9. Send FCM notification to group Admin: "New member joined"
  10. Insert notification log for Admin
  11. Return { success: true, group_id: "uuid", group_name: "..." }

--- EDGE FUNCTION 6: remove-member ---

URL: POST /functions/v1/remove-member

Request body:
{
  "group_id": "uuid",
  "user_id": "uuid"   // the user to remove (not the requesting admin)
}

What it does:
  1. Extract requesting user_id from JWT
  2. Verify requesting user is Admin of group_id
  3. Verify target user_id is an active member of group_id
  4. Verify Admin is not trying to remove themselves
  5. Set group_members.is_active = false for the target user in this group
  6. Update groups.member_count - 1
  7. Send FCM data push to removed member's device(s):
     { "type": "member_removed", "group_id": "uuid", "group_name": "..." }
  8. Insert notification log for removed member (type: 'member_removed')
  9. Return { success: true }

What happens on the removed member's device:
  - FCM handler receives member_removed message
  - App cancels all AlarmManager entries for alarms belonging to this group
  - App deletes all local_alarms rows where group_id matches
  - App removes the group from the groups screen
  - If user is currently on that group's dashboard, navigate them to Groups List

--- EDGE FUNCTION 7: revenuecat-webhook ---

URL: POST /functions/v1/revenuecat-webhook

What it does:
  1. Validate the Authorization header contains the RevenueCat webhook secret
     (stored as an Edge Function secret in Supabase, not hardcoded)
  2. Parse the event type from the webhook body
  3. Handle these event types:
     INITIAL_PURCHASE:
       - Find user by revenuecat_user_id
       - Insert user_subscriptions row with status = 'active'
       - Update users.plan_type to match purchased plan
     RENEWAL:
       - Update user_subscriptions: status = 'active', expires_at = new date
     CANCELLATION:
       - Update user_subscriptions: status = 'cancelled'
       - Do NOT change users.plan_type yet — they keep access until expires_at
     EXPIRATION:
       - Update user_subscriptions: status = 'expired'
       - Update users.plan_type = 'free_trial' (or a new 'expired' state)
       - Note: This means they lose creation rights, not receiving rights
  4. Return HTTP 200 to acknowledge receipt

If the webhook secret is wrong: return 401 immediately, log the attempt.
RevenueCat will retry failed webhooks — the function must be idempotent.

9.5 FIREBASE CLOUD MESSAGING (FCM)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

FCM is used to deliver information to devices in two ways:

TYPE 1: SILENT DATA MESSAGES (no visible notification to user)
  Used for: alarm_sync, alarm_update, alarm_cancel, member_removed
  These are data-only FCM messages with no 'notification' field
  They wake the device's background service (Firebase Messaging headless task)
  The headless task processes the data and takes action (schedule alarm, cancel, etc.)
  The user sees NO notification when these arrive. They are silent.

TYPE 2: DISPLAY NOTIFICATIONS (visible to user in notification tray)
  Used for: all 8 notification types in Section 5.7
  These are FCM messages that cause Notifee to show a notification
  They appear in the Android notification tray
  They are also logged to the notifications table in Supabase

--- FCM PAYLOAD SPECIFICATIONS ---

ALARM SYNC (silent data message):
{
  "data": {
    "type": "alarm_sync",
    "alarm_id": "uuid-string",
    "alarm_time_utc": "2026-06-15T01:00:00Z",
    "difficulty": "medium",
    "sound_url": "https://supabase-url/storage/v1/object/public/audio/...",
    "group_id": "uuid-string",
    "group_name": "Study Group",
    "alarm_title": "Morning Wake Up",
    "is_recurring": "false",
    "recurrence_days": "",         // JSON string of array e.g. "[1,3,5]" or ""
    "recurrence_end_date": ""      // date string or ""
  }
}
Note: All data values in FCM must be strings. Parse numbers/booleans/arrays
in the device-side handler.

ALARM UPDATE (silent data message):
{
  "data": {
    "type": "alarm_update",
    "alarm_id": "uuid-string",
    "alarm_time_utc": "2026-06-15T02:00:00Z",
    "difficulty": "hard",
    "sound_url": "https://...",
    "alarm_title": "Updated Title"
  }
}

ALARM CANCEL (silent data message):
{
  "data": {
    "type": "alarm_cancel",
    "alarm_id": "uuid-string"
  }
}

MEMBER REMOVED (silent data message):
{
  "data": {
    "type": "member_removed",
    "group_id": "uuid-string",
    "group_name": "Study Group"
  }
}

--- FCM BACKGROUND HANDLER (on device) ---

In lib/fcm.ts, register a Firebase Messaging background handler:

  messaging().setBackgroundMessageHandler(async remoteMessage => {
    const data = remoteMessage.data;
    switch (data.type) {
      case 'alarm_sync':
        await handleAlarmSync(data);
        break;
      case 'alarm_update':
        await handleAlarmUpdate(data);
        break;
      case 'alarm_cancel':
        await handleAlarmCancel(data);
        break;
      case 'member_removed':
        await handleMemberRemoved(data);
        break;
    }
  });

This handler runs even when the app is force-killed. It runs as a headless task.

handleAlarmSync(data):
  1. Parse alarm_time_utc → convert to local timezone → alarm_time_local
  2. Upsert row into local SQLite local_alarms table
  3. If sound_url is not empty, download the audio file and save to local file system
     Update local_alarms.sound_path with local file path
  4. Call AlarmManager to schedule: scheduleAlarm(alarm_id, alarm_time_local_ms)
  5. If is_recurring, calculate all future occurrences and schedule each one

handleAlarmUpdate(data):
  1. Cancel existing AlarmManager alarm for this alarm_id
  2. Delete existing local_alarms row for this alarm_id
  3. Re-insert updated row into local_alarms
  4. Reschedule with AlarmManager

handleAlarmCancel(data):
  1. Cancel AlarmManager alarm for this alarm_id
  2. Update local_alarms.status = 'cancelled' (or delete the row)

handleMemberRemoved(data):
  1. Get all alarm_ids from local_alarms where group_id matches
  2. Cancel AlarmManager for each alarm_id
  3. Delete all local_alarms rows where group_id matches
  4. Update local state (groupStore) to remove this group

9.6 ALARM DELIVERY PIPELINE — COMPLETE SEQUENCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is the most important sequence in the entire app. Read it carefully.

PHASE 1: ADMIN CREATES ALARM (requires internet)
  T=0: Admin taps "Save Alarm" in Create Alarm screen
  T=0: App POSTs to create-alarm Edge Function with alarm details
  T=1s: Edge Function inserts alarm into Supabase PostgreSQL
  T=1s: Edge Function creates alarm_completion rows for all members
  T=2s: Edge Function sends FCM data messages to all member devices
  T=2s: App receives { success: true, alarm_id } and shows alarm in Group Dashboard

PHASE 2: DEVICE RECEIVES ALARM DATA (requires internet — happens silently)
  T+few seconds: Member device receives FCM silent data message
  T+few seconds: Firebase Messaging headless task wakes up
  T+few seconds: Handler parses alarm data
  T+few seconds: Writes alarm to local SQLite: INSERT OR REPLACE INTO local_alarms
  T+few seconds: Downloads alarm sound file to local storage (if custom)
  T+few seconds: Calls native AlarmManager.setExactAndAllowWhileIdle()
  → INTERNET IS NO LONGER NEEDED AFTER THIS POINT ←

PHASE 3: ALARM FIRES (works with no internet)
  T=alarm_time: Android OS fires the AlarmManager alarm regardless of:
    - Whether the app is open, closed, or force-killed
    - Whether there is internet connectivity
    - Whether the device is in Doze mode (setExactAndAllowWhileIdle bypasses Doze)
    - Whether the device was rebooted (BootReceiver reschedules after reboot)
  T=alarm_time: AlarmReceiver.java (native) runs
  T=alarm_time: AlarmReceiver calls Notifee to display full-screen notification
  T=alarm_time: AlarmScreen React Native component opens
  T=alarm_time: Volume set to max, alarm sound plays, vibration starts
  T=alarm_time: Math Challenge timer starts (user has 5 minutes)

PHASE 4: CHALLENGE COMPLETION (internet preferred but will retry if offline)
  T=completion: User solves math challenge
  T=completion: Alarm stops, Challenge Success screen shows
  T=completion: App POSTs to complete-challenge Edge Function
    If internet available: completes immediately
    If offline: queued and retried when internet returns (use a simple retry queue)
  T=completion+1s: Edge Function updates Supabase
  T=completion+1s: Supabase Realtime pushes change to Group Dashboard
  T=completion+1s: All group members see this member's status change to green

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9.7 OFFLINE ARCHITECTURE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WAKEIT must work offline. This section explains exactly what works and what
does not work when the device has no internet.

WORKS OFFLINE:
  ✓ Alarm rings (from AlarmManager scheduled in SQLite)
  ✓ Full-screen alarm UI displays
  ✓ Math challenge works (problem generated on-device)
  ✓ App opens and shows cached group/alarm data (from last sync)
  ✓ Alarm countdown timer counts down correctly

DOES NOT WORK OFFLINE (needs internet):
  ✗ New alarms cannot be received (FCM requires internet)
  ✗ Challenge completion cannot be posted (retries when internet returns)
  ✗ Group Dashboard real-time updates do not work
  ✗ Creating/joining groups
  ✗ Scheduling new alarms

When the app detects no internet:
  1. Show the No Internet screen (app/offline)
  2. Display message: "No internet. Your alarms are still scheduled and will ring."
  3. Show "Retry" button that attempts to reconnect
  4. Allow user to dismiss and see cached data (read-only)

RETRY QUEUE for offline completions:
  If complete-challenge POST fails due to no internet:
  1. Store the completion data in a local queue (SQLite or AsyncStorage)
     Queue item: { alarm_id, status, completed_at, attempts, queued_at }
  2. When internet returns (detected via NetInfo), process the queue
  3. Send completions in order (oldest first)
  4. Clear processed items from queue

9.9 SUPABASE REALTIME
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Supabase Realtime is used to push changes to connected clients via WebSocket.
When a row changes in the database, all clients subscribed to that channel
receive the change instantly without polling.

Three channels are used:

CHANNEL 1: group:{group_id}:completions
  Table: alarm_completions
  Filter: group_id = eq.{group_id}
  Events: UPDATE (when a member's status changes from pending to completed/missed)
  Purpose: Group Dashboard updates live when members complete challenges
  Subscribe on: entering Group Dashboard screen
  Unsubscribe on: leaving Group Dashboard screen

CHANNEL 2: group:{group_id}:alarms
  Table: alarms
  Filter: group_id = eq.{group_id}
  Events: INSERT, UPDATE (new alarms or alarm edits)
  Purpose: New alarms appear on Group Dashboard without refresh
  Subscribe on: entering Group Dashboard screen
  Unsubscribe on: leaving Group Dashboard screen

CHANNEL 3: user:{user_id}:notifications
  Table: notifications
  Filter: user_id = eq.{user_id}
  Events: INSERT (new notification)
  Purpose: Update notification badge count on tab bar in real time
  Subscribe on: app foreground (subscribe once when user logs in)
  Unsubscribe on: app logout

How to subscribe (example for channel 1):
  const subscription = supabase
    .channel(`group:${groupId}:completions`)
    .on('postgres_changes', {
      event: 'UPDATE',
      schema: 'public',
      table: 'alarm_completions',
      filter: `group_id=eq.${groupId}`
    }, (payload) => {
      // payload.new contains the updated row
      // Update groupStore / local state to reflect new completion status
    })
    .subscribe();

  // On screen unmount:
  supabase.removeChannel(subscription);

9.11 ROW LEVEL SECURITY (RLS) POLICIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

RLS ensures users can only read and write their own data. Even if an attacker
gets the Supabase anon key, they cannot read another user's data.

TABLE: users
  Policy: SELECT → auth.uid() = id
  Policy: UPDATE → auth.uid() = id

TABLE: user_subscriptions
  Policy: SELECT → user_id = auth.uid()

TABLE: groups
  Policy: SELECT → user is active member OR user is admin
    Condition: EXISTS(
      SELECT 1 FROM group_members 
      WHERE group_id = groups.id 
      AND user_id = auth.uid() 
      AND is_active = true
    )
  Policy: INSERT, UPDATE, DELETE → Edge Function only (service role bypasses RLS)

TABLE: group_members
  Policy: SELECT → user_id = auth.uid() OR user is admin of that group
  Policy: INSERT, UPDATE, DELETE → Edge Function only

TABLE: alarms
  Policy: SELECT → user is active member of the alarm's group
  Policy: INSERT, UPDATE, DELETE → Edge Function only (service role)

TABLE: alarm_completions
  Policy: SELECT → own rows (user_id = auth.uid()) OR user is group admin
  Policy: UPDATE → Edge Function only

TABLE: device_tokens
  Policy: ALL operations → user_id = auth.uid()

TABLE: notifications
  Policy: SELECT → user_id = auth.uid()
  Policy: UPDATE (is_read only) → user_id = auth.uid()
  Policy: INSERT → Edge Function only

11.3 CRITICAL TEST FLOWS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

These 12 tests MUST pass on a real physical Android device before submission.
DO NOT use an emulator — emulators cannot simulate AlarmManager Doze behavior,
actual FCM delivery, or device reboots accurately.

TEST 1: Alarm rings with app force-killed
  Setup: Create alarm 2 minutes in future. Force-kill app (recent apps → swipe away).
  Wait for alarm time.
  Expected: Alarm rings at exact time. Full-screen opens over lock screen.

TEST 2: Alarm rings with no internet
  Setup: Create alarm 2 minutes in future. Turn on airplane mode. Wait.
  Expected: Alarm rings from local AlarmManager/SQLite. No internet required.

TEST 3: Alarm restores after device reboot
  Setup: Create alarm 30 minutes in future. Reboot device. Wait 60 seconds.
  Then wait for alarm time.
  Expected: Alarm rings. BootReceiver rescheduled it within 60 seconds of boot.

TEST 4: Wrong math answer
  Setup: Let alarm ring and open challenge screen. Enter wrong answer.
  Expected: Red feedback, challenge resets with NEW problem, alarm keeps ringing.

TEST 5: Correct math answer
  Setup: Let alarm ring, solve challenge correctly.
  Expected: Sound stops immediately. Vibration stops. Challenge Success screen.
  Supabase alarm_completions row updated to 'completed'.

TEST 6: Challenge timeout (5 minutes)
  Setup: Let alarm ring, DO NOT solve the challenge. Wait 5 minutes.
  Expected: Alarm stops. Alarm Missed screen shows. Supabase record = 'missed'.

TEST 7: Admin cancels alarm
  Setup: Admin creates alarm, waits for FCM sync. Then Admin cancels alarm.
  Expected: FCM cancel push arrives on member devices. AlarmManager entry
  cancelled. local_alarms row updated/deleted.

TEST 8: Admin removes member
  Setup: Admin removes a member from a group.
  Expected: Removed member's app cancels all AlarmManager alarms for that group.
  Removes group from member's Groups List.

TEST 9: Trial expiry
  Setup: Manually set trial_ends_at to a past time in Supabase for a test user.
  Expected: User cannot create groups or alarms. Can still receive alarms from
  groups they were already in.

TEST 10: RevenueCat purchase
  Setup: Use a Google Play test account to purchase a plan.
  Expected: RC webhook fires, Supabase plan_type updates, app entitlements update
  within 60 seconds.

TEST 11: Multiple groups
  Setup: Create 3 groups with different alarm times. Join all 3.
  Expected: All 3 alarms schedule correctly. Each rings at its own time. No
  conflicts between groups.

TEST 12: Recurring alarm
  Setup: Create recurring alarm for Mon/Wed/Fri. Let it ring on first occurrence.
  Expected: Alarm rings. After completion, next occurrence is auto-scheduled.
  Alarm does not ring after recurrence_end_date.
